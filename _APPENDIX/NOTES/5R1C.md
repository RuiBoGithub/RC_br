# 5R1C
在`5R1C`模型中，只有热质量温度(thermal mass temperature, $T_m$)具有热容$C_m$，而空气温度和表面温度均为代数变量(via linear algebraic equations)，无热容。
因此系统的状态变量为：
$x(t) = T_m(t)$
即这是一个一阶系统(1-state system)。

根据`5R1C`模型，热质量节点的连续能量平衡方程如下：
$C_m \frac{dT_m}{dt} = H_{ms}(T_s - T_m) + H_{em}(T_e - T_m) + \phi_m$
其中：
- $C_m$为热质量的热容（J/K）；
- $T_m$为热质量温度（K 或 °C）；
- $T_s$为表面温度（K 或 °C）；
- $T_e$为室外空气温度（K 或 °C）；
- $H_{ms}$为热质量与表面之间的热导（W/K）；
- $H_{em}$为热质量与室外之间的热导（W/K）；
- $\phi_m$为直接作用于热质量的得热（内热源或太阳辐射的一部分，W）。

> 该方程描述了热质量存储能量的变化率等于流入该节点的净热流。
> This equation describes that the rate of change of energy stored in the thermal mass equals the net heat flow into that node.

# Hand-calculation

$C_m \frac{dT_m}{dt} = H,(T_e - T_m) + \Phi$

Where:

* $T_m$ = thermal mass temperature (the state)
* $T_e$ = outdoor temperature (given)
* $H$ = an *equivalent* heat transfer conductance from mass to outside (W/K)
* $\Phi$ = net heat input to the mass (W) (from gains + heating portion)
* $C_m$ = thermal capacitance (J/K)

This is a **first-order linear ODE**.

---

## 1) Pick simple numbers

Let:

* $C_m= 1{,}000{,}000\ \text{J/K}$
* $H= 200 \\text{W/K}$
* $T_e= 5^\circ\text{C}$ (constant)
* $\Phi = 1{,}000\ \text{W}$ (constant)
* Initial condition: $T_m(0)=20^\circ\text{C}$

---

## 2) Put it into standard form

Start with:

$1{,}000{,}000 \frac{dT_m}{dt} = 200(5 - T_m) + 1000$

Expand RHS:

$= 1000 - 200T_m + 1000 = 2000 - 200T_m$

Divide both sides by (1{,}000{,}000):

$\frac{dT_m}{dt} = -0.0002,T_m + 0.002$

This is:

$\frac{dT_m}{dt} = -aT_m + b$
with (a=0.0002\ \text{s}^{-1}), (b=0.002\ ^\circ\text{C/s}).

---

## 3) Solve analytically (closed form)

A standard solution is:

$T_m(t)=T_\infty + \left(T_m(0)-T_\infty\right)e^{-at}$

where the steady-state temperature (T_\infty) is:

$T_\infty=\frac{b}{a}=\frac{0.002}{0.0002}=10^\circ\text{C}$

So:

$T_m(t)=10 + (20-10)e^{-0.0002t}$
$\boxed{T_m(t)=10 + 10,e^{-0.0002t}}$

---

## 4) Compute a value “by hand”: after 1 hour

1 hour = (t=3600) s.

Compute exponent:

$0.0002\times 3600 = 0.72$

$e^{-0.72}\approx 0.4868$

So:

$T_m(3600)=10 + 10(0.4868)=10+4.868= \boxed{14.87^\circ\text{C}}$

Meaning: with cold outdoors and only 1 kW net heating to the mass, the mass cools from 20°C toward 10°C, reaching ~14.9°C in 1 hour.

---

# 5) Compare with doing 1-step numerical update manually (Euler)

Euler update:

$T_{m,k+1} = T_{m,k} + \Delta t\left(-aT_{m,k}+b\right)$

With (T_{m,0}=20), (\Delta t=3600):

Compute derivative at start:

$\dot{T}_m(0)= -0.0002(20)+0.002=-0.004+0.002=-0.002\ ^\circ\text{C/s}$

Then:

$T_{m,1}=20 + 3600(-0.002)=20-7.2=\boxed{12.8^\circ\text{C}}$

Euler gives **12.8°C**, while exact solution gave **14.87°C**.

So with 1-hour step, Euler **over-cools** $too$aggressive), which is exactly why ISO likes Crank–Nicolson.

---

# 6) Same step with Crank–Nicolson (manual, still easy)

Crank–Nicolson for:

$\dot{T}=-aT+b$

is:

$
T_{k+1} = T_k + \frac{\Delta t}{2}\left[(-aT_k+b)+(-aT_{k+1}+b)\right]
$

Rearrange:

$
T_{k+1}\left(1+\frac{a\Delta t}{2}\right)=T_k\left(1-\frac{a\Delta t}{2}\right)+b\Delta t
$


Plug numbers: $a=0.0002,\ \Delta t=3600$

$\frac{a\Delta t}{2}=\frac{0.0002\times 3600}{2}=0.36$

So:

$T_{1}(1+0.36)=T_0(1-0.36)+b\Delta t$
$1.36T_1=20(0.64)+0.002(3600)$
$1.36T_1=12.8+7.2=20.0$
$T_1=\frac{20}{1.36}=\boxed{14.71^\circ\text{C}}$

Crank–Nicolson gives **14.71°C**, very close to the exact **14.87°C**.

---

## Takeaway

* The 5R1C “mass ODE” is usually a **first-order linear ODE**, so you can solve it exactly like above.
* With hourly timesteps, **Euler can be noticeably biased**, while **Crank–Nicolson is much closer** and more stable.
